2023 – 2024 · Leo Club of SLIIT

🌱 EcoSands

Environmental conservation is not achieved through a single event—it is built through continuous action and long-term commitment. That vision inspired EcoSands, a three-phase environmental initiative dedicated to protecting Sri Lanka's coastal and freshwater ecosystems.

🌊 Phase 1: Beach Cleanup & Coconut Plantation

📍 Dehiwala Beach

We began by cleaning the shoreline of Dehiwala Beach, removing waste to create a cleaner environment for both the community and marine life. To contribute to long-term coastal protection, we also planted coconut trees that will help strengthen the shoreline while enhancing the natural landscape.

🌳 Phase 2: Kumbuk Plantation

📍 Around Thalangama Lake

Our journey continued with the planting of 50 Kumbuk (Terminalia arjuna) trees around Thalangama Lake. These native trees play a vital role in preserving biodiversity, reducing soil erosion, and protecting valuable wetland ecosystems for future generations.

🌿 Phase 3: Kadolana Mangrove Plantation

📍 Kalido Beach

In the final phase, we planted 100+ Kadolana mangrove plants at Kalido Beach. Mangroves are among the world's most effective natural coastal defenses, providing habitats for wildlife, preventing coastal erosion, improving water quality, and helping combat climate change by storing significant amounts of carbon.

Every tree planted and every piece of waste collected represents a step toward a greener, cleaner, and more sustainable Sri Lanka. Together, we demonstrated that collective action can create lasting environmental change.

A heartfelt thank you to every volunteer, partner, and supporter who dedicated their time and effort to making EcoSands a success. Your commitment proves that meaningful change begins with people who choose to act.

Let's continue protecting our environment—for our communities, our wildlife, and future generations. 💚🌍
